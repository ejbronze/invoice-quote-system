About SantoSync

SantoSync

SantoSync is a coined name designed to feel steady, premium, and synchronized for modern trade operations.

SantoSync is a focused document and operations workspace built for trade teams, freelancers, and logistics operators who need to generate, track, and deliver professional quotes and invoices without the overhead of enterprise billing software.

What it does
  • Creates, saves, and exports branded quotes and invoices as print-ready PDFs
  • Tracks payments per invoice using a built-in ledger with balance and history
  • Organizes clients with multi-contact profiles and per-client financial stats
  • Generates Statements of Account for client reconciliation and payment follow-up
  • Provides reporting dashboards for pipeline value, outstanding balances, and client aging
  • Supports structured payment terms — Net 15, Net 30, custom days, or Due Immediately
  • Runs fully in the browser with server-backed shared workspace data — no install needed
Problems it solves
  • Getting professional quotes and invoices out quickly without bloated software
  • Knowing exactly what has been billed versus collected, per client, at a glance
  • Producing print-ready PDFs with your own branding — no design tool required
  • Catching overdue invoices and client balance exposure before they become collection issues
  • Sharing a live workspace across a small team without complex setup or per-seat licensing
Why it exists

Most invoicing tools are either too lightweight to handle real trade workflows or too heavy for teams that just need clean documents, accurate records, and fast exports. SantoSync was built to sit in that gap — a workspace that feels like a real operational tool, not a stripped-down accounting sidebar. It prioritizes speed, document fidelity, and a UI that gets out of the way.

Designed and developed by Edwin Jaquez through Palmchat Innovations Lab under Palmchat Innovations LLC NYC, with a focus on premium operational tools for modern trade and logistics teams.

Getting Started

How do I create a new quote or invoice?

Click the + New button at the top of the Documents page, or use the + New dropdown on the dashboard. Choose Quote, Invoice, or Statement. The editor opens in a step-by-step flow — fill in document details, client info, line items, keywords, then review before exporting.

How do I edit an existing document?

Click the pencil icon on any document card, or click Edit inside the PDF preview window. The editor reopens with all fields intact and ready to update.

Edit button Edit
How do I set up my company profile?

Open Settings from the sidebar, then click Company Profile. Your name, address, logo, letterhead, and payment info will appear on every document you generate.

Documents & Exporting

What is the difference between a Quote and an Invoice?

A Quote is a price estimate sent before work begins. An Invoice is a formal payment request issued after work is completed or goods are delivered. You can switch between the two in Step 1 of the editor.

How do I export a document to PDF?

Click the eye icon on any document card to open the PDF preview window, then use your browser's print dialog to save as PDF. You can also reach the preview from Step 6 – Final Preview inside the editor.

Open PDF preview Preview PDF
How do I add line items to a quote or invoice?

Step 3 shows a spreadsheet-style table where each row is one line item. Click + Add Item to add a new row, then fill in the Description, Quantity, Unit Price, and Total inline. All fields are visible at once — no expanding or collapsing needed. Use Tab to move between columns; pressing Tab on the last field of the last row automatically adds a new row. Press Enter on any numeric field to advance to the next column, or use Cmd/Ctrl+Enter from anywhere to add a new row immediately.

Can I save line items to reuse later?

Yes. Reusable items live in the Pricing Library. Click an item to review its details, then use Edit Item inside the detail modal when you need to update pricing, supplier, packaging, image, or notes. The quote and invoice line-item table stays focused on direct entry.

What are Internal Notes and how are they different from regular Notes?

The Notes field in Step 3 appears on the printed document and is visible to your client. Internal Notes (marked with a "Private" badge) are saved with the document but never appear in PDF exports or printed output — they are for your team only. Use Internal Notes to log submission dates, follow-up reminders, payment context, or any operational detail. A brief preview of internal notes appears on the document card in the list view so your team can see them at a glance without opening the editor.

How do I set payment terms on an invoice?

Scroll to the Document Notes & Terms panel at the bottom of Step 3. The Terms of Payment selector gives you four options: Due Immediately (greys out all other options and prints "Payment is due immediately upon receipt"), Net 15 (due date auto-set to 15 days from invoice date), Net 30 (due date auto-set to 30 days), or Other — where you enter a custom day count and optional terms text. Invoice cards in the document list show the calculated due date, the payment term label, and an overdue indicator when applicable.

How do I back up or restore my data?

Open Settings from the sidebar and use the JSON Backup option to export a full workspace snapshot, including documents, clients, saved items, statements, and admin data. Use JSON Restore to import a previously saved backup. The server also keeps timestamped recovery snapshots, and CSV import remains available for bulk document creation.

Document Cards

What do the action icons on document cards do?

Each card shows two icon buttons on the right. The eye icon opens the PDF preview in a new window. The pencil icon opens the document in the editor. A three-dot menu gives access to additional actions like payment status, type conversion, and delete.

Card action buttons Preview PDF Edit
How do I use the PDF preview window?

The preview window shows the print-ready document. Use Print to open your browser's print dialog and save as PDF. Use Edit to jump back to the editor with the document loaded. Close Preview dismisses the window.

How do I filter documents by type?

Use the All / Quotes / Invoices / Statements tabs directly below the search bar. Clicking a tab shows only documents of that type in the same view — no page reload. The Statements tab shows previously generated Statement of Account exports.

How do I mark an invoice as paid?

On the Documents page, open the invoice card's three-dot menu and select the appropriate payment status — Unpaid, Pending, or Paid. For real payment tracking, open the invoice and use the payment ledger, or log a payment from the Statements page. Paid invoices are visually flagged and excluded from outstanding balance calculations in Invoice Reports.

Clients & Statements

How do I save a client for reuse?

In Step 2 of the editor, enter the client name and address, then click Save Client. Saved clients appear in the dropdown on future documents so you don't need to retype their details. Switching clients also restores their saved consignee fields.

What is a Statement of Account?

A Statement of Account is a branded PDF summary of selected invoices for a specific client — useful for reconciliation and payment follow-up. Access it from the Invoice Reports section. Generated statements are saved and appear under the Statements tab on the home screen.

How are client outstanding balances calculated?

Client balances come from that client's invoices. Paid invoices count as 0 outstanding, while Pending or Unpaid invoices count the remaining invoice total after logged payments. Changing an invoice from Pending to Paid reduces the client balance immediately; changing it back to Pending or Unpaid adds the unpaid amount back immediately.

What do the action icons on statement rows do?

Each saved statement has three action buttons. The eye icon reopens the PDF export. The pencil icon opens the statement editor so you can adjust it. The trash icon permanently deletes the saved statement.

Statement action buttons Open Edit Delete
How do I delete a logged payment?

Go to the Statements page and expand the payment inside Payment History. Use Delete Payment to remove it. SantoSync now opens a confirmation modal before deleting logged payments, and the invoice balance plus reporting totals refresh immediately after the removal. If you are still editing an invoice, removing a payment from the invoice ledger uses the same confirmation step.

Tips & Shortcuts

How do I switch the display language?

Use the language selector in the sidebar footer (EN / ES / FR). The interface and generated document labels will update to match your selected language immediately.

Can I add my company logo and signature to documents?

Yes. Open Settings from the sidebar, then click Company Profile. You can upload a letterhead image, signature, stamp, and footer wave — all of which appear in exported PDFs automatically.

How do I search for a document?

Use the search bar at the top of the Documents page. You can search by client name, reference number, date, or any keyword. The same query simultaneously filters whichever tab is active — including the Statements tab, which is indexed by client name, vendor, reference number, and date.

How do I use the Catalog?

Click Pricing Library in the sidebar. Use search, category, supplier, image filters, sorting, and the Card/List toggle to browse reusable items captured from quotes, invoices, and procurement rows. The page shows 25 items per page by default, with 50 and 100 item options. Click any item to open its detail modal; editing starts only from the Edit Item action inside that modal.

How does Pricing Library pagination work?

The Pricing Library shows a range such as 1-25 of 143 and page controls below the results. Choose 25, 50, or 100 items per page. Search or filter changes reset back to page 1 so the results stay predictable.

What is the auto-cycling card on the dashboard?

The snapshot card in the top-right of the dashboard automatically rotates every 3 seconds between three metrics: Pipeline Value (blue), Amount Invoiced (green), and Income Received (amber). Click the card at any time to advance manually; the 3-second timer resets from that point.

Can I set a payment due date automatically?

Yes. In Step 3 under Document Notes & Terms, select Net 15 or Net 30 and the due date on the invoice card is calculated automatically from the invoice date — no manual entry needed. Choosing Other lets you enter any number of days and an optional custom terms line. Check Due Immediately to mark the invoice payable on receipt, which overrides the date selector entirely.

How do I report a bug or request a feature?

Click Submit / Report Issues in the footer. Fill in a summary, add any relevant screenshots, and submit. Your report is logged and visible to the admin in the Service Reports panel under Settings.
